How do we do our paternity DNA testing?

Our paternity tests look at up to 42 DNA markers and examine the child’s DNA and identify which half is inherited from the mother and which half is from the father. Ideally, we would prefer the mother’s sample as well as the father’s, but we can still perform the paternity test without the mother’s sample.

When testing the biological father of the child both will share identical sections of DNA at each marker. When the tested man is not the biological father there will be differences in the DNA of the man and the child.
